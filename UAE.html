The Effects of Oil in The United Arab Emirates

Since the 1950s, when oil was discovered, the United Arab Emirates (UAE) have undergone significant changes. The nation's rich oil deposits have had a big impact on the social, economic, and environmental aspects of life there. The abundant reserves of oil have not only propelled the UAE to become one of the wealthiest nations globally but have also triggered multifaceted effects that have reverberated throughout the country. The UAE's economic growth has been based primarily on the finding of oil. The UAE's GDP grew by 348.5%, from USD 103 billion in 2001 to USD 359 billion in 2020, primarily due to its oil sector. With the help of oil exports, the UAE's economy has grown quickly, making it one of the richest countries in the world compared to a mostly desert-based economy. The UAE has been able to diversify its economy, build a strong social system, and invest in infrastructure development because to the money it receives from oil exports. In 2009 85% of the UAE's economy was based off of oil exports as well as accounting for 77% of the UAE’s state budget. Simply digesting these numbers should paint a picture of the sheer importance that oil has on the economy. In addition, the oil business has drawn knowledge and capital from abroad, which has aided in the expansion of a number of industries, including finance, real estate, and tourism.

In the UAE, the oil industry's contribution to economic success has had a significant societal impact. Living standards, healthcare, and education have all significantly improved as a result of the wealth inflow. Through significant investments in social welfare programs, the government has given its citizens access to high-quality homes, work opportunities, healthcare, and education. Furthermore, the cosmopolitan society of the United Arab Emirates has drawn a wide population of expatriates, resulting in a mixed environment that promotes tolerance and variety. Although oil has significantly increased income and prosperity in the United Arab Emirates, it has also created serious environmental problems. The UAE is one of the worlds largest oil producers and home to seven so-called “carbon bombs' '. Carbon bombs are the world's largest fossil fuel projects, the UAE is producing and burning fossil fuels at a rate the questions of climate change implications must be asked. The extraction, processing, and combustion of oil have resulted in contamination of the air and water, devastation of habitats, and emissions of greenhouse gasses into the atmosphere. Moving away from oil as the main energy source has also made it more difficult to switch to renewable and sustainable energy sources. The UAE has made investments in renewable energy programs, sustainable development projects, and conservation activities in an effort to lessen the environmental impact of these difficulties.

For the United Arab Emirates, the oil finding has been a double-edged sword: it has brought them unheard-of levels of economic success but also presented environmental difficulties. The UAE must find a balance between maximizing the benefits of its oil wealth and reducing its negative environmental repercussions as it moves forward with its goal of economic diversification and sustainable development. The United Arab Emirates has the potential to construct a robust and sustainable future that guarantees prosperity for future generations by means of innovative approaches, international cooperation, and strategic planning
